Vitaly Sumin: L.A. Auteur

16 May 2018

Russian Oceanographer turned L.A. auteur, Vitaly Sumin isn't making waves in Hollywood, but his movies ripple rapturously around the world.

Vitaly Sumin is a Los Angeles based filmmaker whose work is inspired by the writings of Russian philosopher Fyodor Dostoevski. Sumin's films are niche to the extreme and are produced with virtually no budget. Yet, his work has won numerous awards, making the man a modern day, lo-fi, auteur.

Sumin's company, VM Productions, released Dostoevski Reimagined: Notes from the New World in 2011 and are currently working on a related project. The Making of Notes from the New World, is a behind-the-scenes account of the how the original movie was almost never completed, "a tsunami of unforeseen challenges threatened to tear these Notes to shreds".

Both movies star Natasha Blasick, a well established actress who has played parts in numerous movies including Paranoid Activity 2 (2011) and more recently the TV series Jean-Claude Van Johnson (2017). Blasick has also recently announced that she will be involved in Sumin's next movie project Crime and Punishment, LA, based on Dostoevski's book - Crime and Punishment.

The movies that Sumin has to his name, there are four that have been completed, are a refreshing change from the mainstream and act as a reminder that the L.A. scene is not just a playground for powerful studio executives.

As for our part, Phiz Digital are currently managing the VM Productions Instagram account and are soon to take on it's YouTube channel. The brief is simple enough, spread the word and help Sumin raise the funds he needs to complete The Making of Notes from the New World, currently in post-production.
